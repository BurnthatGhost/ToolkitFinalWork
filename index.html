What is Modernity?

Modernity is the era since the Age of Enlightenment when the "new" world system was created.

It is a conception of time as a continuous progressive, purposeful and irreversible development.

Why is it modernity?

Modernity continues to the present day and encompasses the developments represented by postmodernity.

Features of postmodernity include globalization, consumerism, the breakdown of authority and the commodification of knowledge.

What to do with modernity?

The rationalism that formed the base of modern thinking in understanding and imagining the world, banished the ignorance of the religious age, and propelled mankind irreversibly towards the modern age has also posed great challenges to mankind.

If one believes in Socrates' maxim that "the unreflected life is not worth living," then we can also build on this by continuing to ask, is life after a radical introspection bearable?
